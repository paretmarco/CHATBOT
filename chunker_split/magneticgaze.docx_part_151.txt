[69] Bartolus di Sassoferrato (+1357), one of the most celebrious jurists of the Middle Ages, talks of a witch from Orta di Riparia within the diocesis of Novara, in the process asserting that “this womam was able, with her eye, to cause damage to humans or animals and to kill them”. This represents the first testimony attempting to carry out a juristic analaysis of the effects of the look, one which additionally reveals the existence of an uninterrupted historical continuity as regards the occurrence in some localities of a certain category of events.
