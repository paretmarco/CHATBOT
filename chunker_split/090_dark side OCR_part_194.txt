Conclusion Sensory distortion, the slowing down or speeding up of sensa- tions, is a creative act of the observer. Once the sensory distortion is seen for what it is, with no judgments, labels, or evaluation, the observer can take over its creative function, which it had given to the inner child. The inner child automatically had contracted or expanded sensations. Now the observer can own its creative side and exist in present time.
